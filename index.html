Quizery

http://quizery.herokuapp.com
https://github.com/jsimon727/quizery

Quizery is a web application that allows teachers to create custom online quizzes for their students. Teachers can specify a start date, set a time limit, and select a specific category for each quiz. Students can access and submit quizzes to receive a score. They can then create an account to save all their quiz scores and view their progress over time. This application was built with Ruby on Rails, JQuery, Javascript, HTML, and CSS.

All Quizzes

Users have access to all quizzes that have been created by various teachers. They can select a quiz to complete by clicking on its associated button. They also have the ability to search for a specific quiz using the search bar.

How It Works

This section explains how the site operates in a simple, easy to read way, enhancing the user experience. The bright blue contrast makes the section stand out as well as gives the site a playful feel.

Creation Zone

This section is only accessible by teachers. Teachers can create completely customized quizzes that have multiple questions and answers. They can also set a quiz start date and time limit.

Quiz

Students can take each quiz in the allotted time specified by the teacher. They can not access the quiz until the start date. Once they submit the quiz it will be automatically graded and their score will show on the screen.

Track Progress

Students can track their quiz progress over time. Each quiz is listed with their score as well as a bar graph, providing a visual representation of their overall progress. This allows students to easily pinpoint where they need to improve.

GetHappy

http://gethappy.herokuapp.com
https://github.com/jsimon727/gethappy

GetHappy is a happy hour location aggregator that allows users to search by either their current location or favorite locations to find the best happy hours. GetHappy displays the closest happy hour location as well as the associated reviews and location information. This happy hour finder also lets users find sweet deals in their area!

User Log In

Users can log in or sign-up for the site. I used the Bcrypt-Ruby gem to ensure authentication along with validation to ensure the user information is unique upon sign-up.

User Profile

Users can view their favorite bars and frequently visited locations. They can find happy hour locations closest to their current location with the click of a button.

Local Happy Hours

These are the closest happy hour locations. They are pulled from the Yelp API and displayed for the user. The information includes the bar name, location, and reviews.

Local Deals

The closest happy hour deals are listed with additional deal details and exclusions. They are pulled from the 8Coupon API and displayed in an easy to read format.

Acceptance Test Example

Each feature has an associated acceptance test, making sure the user experience is seamless. I used Capybara and Rspec to execute the tests.

CardMe

http://getcarded.herokuapp.com
https://github.com/jsimon727/getcarded

CardMe is a digital business card solution. It allows users to sign up, create their own custom business cards, and send these cards to others via text or email. Users can add business cards to their rolodex and view company information associated with that card. This was a group project completed during the Web Development Immersive Program at General Assembly.

User Log In

This is the main page where users can join or log into their accounts. The log in form is sleek and simple to use. Bcrypt is used to ensure authentication.

How It Works

This section gives an overview of how the site functions. It was designed to simply explain the site to new users and catch their attention when they land on the page.

Create a Business Card

Users can use this form to create their own custom business cards. The Paperclip gem and AWS storage allows users to upload a profile picture image as well as a background image.

Send Card

Users can email or text their cards to others. The Twilio and ActionMailer gems along with the use of tokens allow users to send unique links to their connections.

Rolodex

Users can store their connection's business cards here and click the arrow to see company information and news stories, as well as the plus sign to add a card to a specific group.
